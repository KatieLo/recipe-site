Sticky Toffee Pudding

A classic winter warmer, sticky toffee pudding is the perfect end to a cosy winter supper. This version is a grown-up pud with deep fruity flavours, but for a classic steamed sponge just leave out the fruits and nuts.

Main Ingredients

  • 160g demarera sugar
  • 130g unsalted butter at room tempereture
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • 1 large egg
  • 250 plain flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 300ml water
  • 1 mashed banana
  • 200 pitted whole dried dates
  • 50g currants
  • 50g sultanas
  • 100g chopped walnuts
  • 1T bicarbonate of soda

Caramel Sauce

  • 250g castor sugar
  • 100ml water
  • 100ml orange juice
  • 300ml double cream

Method

Set the oven to 180° celsius and grease six 300ml ramekins. Cream the sugar and butter until pale and fluffy. Add the vanilla essence and egg and beat again for one minute. Sift the flour and baking powder together, and add to the butter mix. Mix well and set aside in a warm place.

Put the water, fruit and nuts in a pan and bring to the boil. Remove from the heat and stir in the bicarbonate of soda. Don't worry about the frothing! Stir this into the batter mixture and mix well. Spoon into the ramekins until they are 3/4 full and place on a baking trayin the oven. Check them after 25 minutes by poking a skewer or knife in, and if it comes out clean remove them. Be careful not to overcook - they should be moist, even to the side of gooey. Once cooked, let the puddings sit in the ramekins for ten minutes before turning out.

Caramel:

Pour the caramel sauce over the puddings and serve with vanilla ice cream, custard or whipped cream.
